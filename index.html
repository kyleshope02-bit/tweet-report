📖 What the Numbers Mean
Strike
The price your shares get sold at if the stock closes above it at expiry. You keep the premium no matter what — but shares get called away at this price.
Est. Premium
Cash you collect per share when you sell the call. e.g. $2.80 x 400 shares (4 contracts) = $1,120 into your account immediately. Yours to keep regardless.
Downside To
How far the stock can fall before you are net negative after collecting the premium. Formula: Cost Basis minus Premium. e.g. AMZN cost basis $213.21 minus $2.80 premium = $210.41. Above that price at expiry, you profit on the combined position.
Ann. Ret %
Annualised yield of the premium, based on current stock price. Formula: (Premium divided by Stock Price) x (365 divided by DTE) x 100. e.g. $2.80 / $213.49 x (365/40) x 100 = 12.0%. Anything above 10% annualised is strong for a covered call — you are still long the stock the whole time.
OTM %
How far the strike is above the current price. Higher = safer but less premium. Lower = more income but shares more likely to get called away. Sweet spot: 5-10% OTM.
Total
Premium x 100 x contracts. This cash hits your account the moment the trade fills.
📞 How to Execute These Trades
Step 1 Pick your tier
Conservative if you want to hold the stock long-term. Balanced for best income/upside tradeoff. Aggressive if you're happy being called away at the strike price.
Step 2 Place the order
In your broker: Sell → Call → [strike] → [expiry] → Limit order at the bid price or midpoint. Select 'Covered Call' — you don't need extra margin.
Step 3 Set your management rule
If the option reaches 2× what you collected (e.g. you sold for $4, close if it hits $8), buy it back to cut losses. At 50% profit (e.g. sold $4, hits $2), consider closing early and selling a new one.
