📋 What We Look For — Scoring Criteria
IV / HV RATIO  (30 pts)
Implied Volatility vs Historical Volatility.
▲ >130 = IV highly elevated — market is overpaying for options, ideal for selling premium.
▶ 100–130 = IV above HV — still good.
▼ <100 = IV below HV — thin premium, avoid.
Higher IV = fatter premiums for the same strike distance.
ANNUALISED RETURN  (25 pts)
Premium collected ÷ capital at risk, scaled to a yearly rate so all DTEs are comparable.
▲ >40% = excellent.
▶ 25–40% = good.
▶ 12–25% = acceptable.
A 2% return in 30 days = 24% annualised — better than most savings rates.
DAYS TO EXPIRATION — DTE  (20 pts)
How many days until the option expires.
▲ 21–45 days = sweet spot — theta decay accelerates, risk is manageable.
▶ 14–55 days = acceptable.
▼ <14 or >55 = outside ideal range.
Options lose value fastest in the last 30 days — that works in the seller's favour.
OTM BUFFER  (15 pts)
How far Out-of-The-Money the strike is vs current price.
▲ 5–15% OTM = ideal — enough buffer to survive a pullback, still meaningful premium.
▶ 3–20% OTM = acceptable.
▼ <3% = too close, high assignment risk.
CSP: you want the stock to stay above the strike. CC: you want it to stay below.
PUT / CALL RATIO  (10 pts)
Volume of puts traded vs calls — a market sentiment gauge.
▲ <0.8 = bullish sentiment — good for CSPs (stock less likely to fall).
▶ 0.8–1.2 = neutral.
▼ >1.2 = bearish pressure — caution on CSPs, avoid CCs.
High put volume = traders hedging downside = stock may be under pressure.

CC Management Rules
50% Profit — Buy Back
CC worth 50% of sale price
When your sold call has decayed to half its value, buy it back. You keep the other 50% and can immediately sell a new CC further out. This is the core CC management rule — capturing most of theta without riding gamma risk to zero.
2× Premium — Close the CC
CC worth 2× what you collected
If the stock rips through your strike and your CC doubles in price, buy it back and reassess. Don't let an uncapped loss develop on a covered call — the stock is telling you something. You still own the shares; you're just removing the call obligation.
Sell 30-45 DTE, Close at 21
Sweet spot for covered calls
Sell new CCs at 30-45 DTE where theta decay is steepest. Close or roll when 21 DTE remains — gamma accelerates inside 21 days and your CC becomes harder to manage. Always be rolling into the next 30-45 DTE window.
Roll Up & Out for Net Credit
Stock approaches your strike
If your stock is approaching the call strike, roll UP (higher strike) and OUT (later expiry). The new CC must generate a net credit — new premium minus buyback cost must be positive. Never pay a debit to roll a covered call. If you can't roll for credit, let it assign or close.
Strike Selection: 4-8% OTM
Sell OTM calls on stocks you own
For covered calls, 4-8% OTM is the sweet spot: enough premium to be meaningful, far enough that you keep most upside if the stock runs. Aggressive (1-3% OTM) = more income, higher assignment risk. Conservative (8-12% OTM) = less income, stock can run further before being called away.
Never Sell CCs Into Earnings
Earnings within expiry window
IV spikes before earnings then collapses after — selling a CC expiring after earnings means you collect inflated premium but face the full post-earnings move. Always sell CCs expiring BEFORE the earnings date, as you've been doing.
