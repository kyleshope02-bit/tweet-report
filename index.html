📋 What We Look For — Scoring Criteria
IV / HV RATIO  (30 pts)
Implied Volatility vs Historical Volatility.
▲ >130 = IV highly elevated — market is overpaying for options, ideal for selling premium.
▶ 100–130 = IV above HV — still good.
▼ <100 = IV below HV — thin premium, avoid.
Higher IV = fatter premiums for the same strike distance.
ANNUALISED RETURN  (25 pts)
Premium collected ÷ capital at risk, scaled to a yearly rate so all DTEs are comparable.
▲ >40% = excellent.
▶ 25–40% = good.
▶ 12–25% = acceptable.
A 2% return in 30 days = 24% annualised — better than most savings rates.
DAYS TO EXPIRATION — DTE  (20 pts)
How many days until the option expires.
▲ 21–45 days = sweet spot — theta decay accelerates, risk is manageable.
▶ 14–55 days = acceptable.
▼ <14 or >55 = outside ideal range.
Options lose value fastest in the last 30 days — that works in the seller's favour.
OTM BUFFER  (15 pts)
How far Out-of-The-Money the strike is vs current price.
▲ 5–15% OTM = ideal — enough buffer to survive a pullback, still meaningful premium.
▶ 3–20% OTM = acceptable.
▼ <3% = too close, high assignment risk.
CSP: you want the stock to stay above the strike. CC: you want it to stay below.
PUT / CALL RATIO  (10 pts)
Volume of puts traded vs calls — a market sentiment gauge.
▲ <0.8 = bullish sentiment — good for CSPs (stock less likely to fall).
▶ 0.8–1.2 = neutral.
▼ >1.2 = bearish pressure — caution on CSPs, avoid CCs.
High put volume = traders hedging downside = stock may be under pressure.
