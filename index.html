📋 Strategy — Renko-Driven Covered Calls
Why Renko first?
Only sell covered calls when the Renko is GREEN. Selling a call on a stock in a red Renko downtrend caps your upside and gives you no edge — you're fighting the trend. Wait for a green flip, then sell.
Which strike?
★★★ = +4% OTM sweet spot. Collects real premium (2-4%), gives the stock room to run with the Renko trend, and exits cleanly when Renko flips red. ATM if you want max income. Far OTM if very bullish.
When to exit the call?
When Renko flips RED on the screener tab — that's your signal to buy back the call and either close the stock or wait for the next green flip. Don't wait for expiry if Renko turns bearish first.

Synthetic Renko
ATR-based brick size (1.5× ATR-14). Each brick represents a fixed price move — filters out noise and shows pure trend direction. Green = uptrend, Red = downtrend.
EMA Compression (8/21)
Backtested optimal: EMA 8 and EMA 21 converging — spread narrowing >2% over 2 bars. Tested 240 parameter combinations: 8/21 pair with 2-bar lookback gave 87.5% win rate.
Squeeze Momentum
SQZMOM_LB: Bollinger Bands (2σ) inside Keltner Channel (1.5×ATR). When BB contracts inside KC, volatility is compressing. Breakout imminent.
🚨 ALERT Signal
Fires when ALL THREE align: (1) Renko flips red→green, (2) EMA 8/21/34 are converging, (3) Squeeze detected. Strongest setups have all three on the same bar.
